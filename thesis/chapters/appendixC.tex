\chapter{Transaction Costs} \label{app:transaction_costs}

Following the methodology outlined by \cite{SparseAndStableInternationalPortfolioOptimization},  \cite{SparseAndStableInternationalPortfolioOptimization} and \cite{Li2015}, and keeping the notation introduced in Chapter \ref{chap:model-free-returns}, we model transaction costs as proportional to the volume of traded assets and currencies, ignoring any fixed trading costs. The return of the hedged portfolio is expressed as the sum of unhedged asset returns and gains from forward currency positions
\begin{equation}
R^{h}_{P,t+1} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} x_{i,t} R^{u}_{i,t+1} + \sum_{c=1}^{M} \phi_{c,t} (f_{c,t} - e_{c,t+1}). \tag{\eqref{eq:hedged-portfolio-return}}
\end{equation}

Let $\tau^{asset}_{t+1}$ and $\tau^{curr}_{t+1}$ denote the total transaction costs associated with asset and currency trades, respectively. The portfolio return net of transaction costs is then given by
\begin{equation}
R^{h,net}_{P,t+1} = R^{h}_{P,t+1} - \tau^{asset}_{t+1} - \tau^{curr}_{t+1}.
\label{eq:hedged-portfolio-return-net}
\end{equation}
 
The total transaction costs are computed as the sum of the costs incurred from trading individual assets and currencies, weighted by the respective trading volumes
\begin{equation*}
\tau^{asset}_{t+1} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \tau^{asset}_{i,t+1} |x_{i,t+1} - x^{-}_{i,t+1}|,
\end{equation*}
\begin{equation*}
\tau^{curr}_{t+1} = \sum_{c=1}^{M} \tau^{curr}_{c,t+1}  \phi_{c,t+1} + \sum_{c=1}^{M} \tau^{curr}_{c,t+1} |w_{c,t+1} - w^{-}_{c,t+1}|,
\end{equation*}
where $\tau^{asset}_{i,t+1}$ and $\tau^{curr}_{c,t+1}$ are the per-unit transaction costs for asset $i$ and currency $c$. The terms $x^{-}_{i,t+1}$ and $w^{-}_{c,t+1}$ denote the asset weights and implied currency weights immediately prior to rebalancing at time $t+1$ (i.e., the drifted weights).

Asset transaction costs are incurred exclusively when rebalancing individual asset holdings. Currency transaction costs, however, arise from two sources, adjustments in forward hedge positions (assuming the forward contract maturity matches the rebalancing frequency, the old contract expires and a new contract of full notional size $\phi_{c,t+1}$ must be established at each rebalancing date) and currency exchanges required to fund purchases of assets denominated in different currencies ($|w_{c,t+1} - w^{-}_{c,t+1}|$). If the portfolio is rebalanced more frequently than the forward contract maturity, existing forward contracts are kept open and only incremental adjustments are traded. In this case, the forward transaction cost term is modeled as the absolute difference, replacing $\sum_{c=1}^{M} \tau^{curr}_{c,t+1} \phi_{c,t+1}$ with $\sum_{c=1}^{M} \tau^{curr}_{c,t+1} |\phi_{c,t+1} - \phi^{-}_{c,t+1}|$, where $\phi^{-}_{c,t+1}$ represents the drifted hedge weight. Following \cite{SparseAndStableInternationalPortfolioOptimization}, these costs are aggregated at the currency level, operating under the assumption that proceeds from selling assets denominated in currency $c$ can fund the purchase of other assets in the same currency without incurring currency conversion fees.

To define the individual per-unit costs, we consider an asset $i$ denominated in currency $c_i$. Let $P^{ask}_{i,t}$, $P^{bid}_{i,t}$, and $P_{i,t}$ represent the ask, bid, and mid prices of the asset, while $S^{ask}_{c_i,t}$, $S^{bid}_{c_i,t}$, and $S_{c_i,t}$ denote the corresponding currency prices. If asset $i$ is purchased at time $t$ and sold at time $t+1$, its realized log return is given by
\begin{align*}
\log \left( \frac{P^{bid}_{i,t+1}S^{bid}_{c_i,t+1}}{P^{ask}_{i,t} S^{ask}_{c_i,t}} \right) 
&= \log \left( \frac{ \left( P_{i,t+1} - \frac{\delta^{asset}_{i,t+1}}{2} \right) \left( S_{c_i,t+1} - \frac{\delta^{curr}_{c_i,t+1}}{2} \right) }{ \left( P_{i,t} + \frac{\delta^{asset}_{i,t}}{2} \right) \left( S_{c_i,t} + \frac{\delta^{curr}_{c_i,t}}{2} \right) } \right) \\
&= \log \left( \frac{ P_{i,t+1} \left( 1 - \frac{\delta^{asset}_{i,t+1}}{2P_{i,t+1}} \right) S_{c_i,t+1} \left( 1 - \frac{\delta^{curr}_{c_i,t+1}}{2S_{c_i,t+1}} \right) }{ P_{i,t} \left( 1 + \frac{\delta^{asset}_{i,t}}{2P_{i,t}} \right) S_{c_i,t} \left( 1 + \frac{\delta^{curr}_{c_i,t}}{2S_{c_i,t}} \right) } \right) \\
&= \log \left( \frac{P_{i,t+1}S_{c_i,t+1}}{P_{i,t}S_{c_i,t}} \right) + \log \left( \frac{ 1 - \frac{\delta^{asset}_{i,t+1}}{2P_{i,t+1}} }{ 1 + \frac{\delta^{asset}_{i,t}}{2P_{i,t}} } \right) + \log \left( \frac{ 1 - \frac{\delta^{curr}_{c_i,t+1}}{2S_{c_i,t+1}} }{ 1 + \frac{\delta^{curr}_{c_i,t}}{2S_{c_i,t}} } \right) \\
&= \log \left( \frac{P_{i,t+1}S_{c_i,t+1}}{P_{i,t}S_{c_i,t}} \right) - \log \left( \frac{1 + \frac{\delta^{asset}_{i,t}}{2P_{i,t}}}{1 - \frac{\delta^{asset}_{i,t+1}}{2P_{i,t+1}}} \right) - \log \left( \frac{1 + \frac{\delta^{curr}_{c_i,t}}{2S_{c_i,t}}}{1 - \frac{\delta^{curr}_{c_i,t+1}}{2S_{c_i,t+1}}} \right),
\end{align*}
where $\delta^{asset}_{i}$ and $\delta^{curr}_{c_i}$ are the bid-ask spreads. Comparing this to equation \eqref{eq:hedged-portfolio-return-net}, the transaction cost is precisely the difference between the actual realized log return and mid-price returs for the asset and currency. 

Defining half of the proportional bid-ask spreads as 
\begin{equation*}
\chi^{asset}_{i,t} = \frac{P^{ask}_{i,t} - P^{bid}_{i,t}}{2P_{i,t}} \quad \text{and} \quad \chi^{curr}_{c,t} = \frac{S^{ask}_{c,t} - S^{bid}_{c,t}}{2S_{c,t}},
\end{equation*}
the the per-unit transaction costs for trading asset $i$ and currency $c$ become
\begin{equation*}
\tau^{asset}_{i,t+1} = 
\begin{cases}
\log \left( \frac{1 + \chi^{asset}_{i,t+1}}{1 - \chi^{asset}_{i,t}} \right) & \text{if } x_{i,t+1} > x^{-}_{i,t+1}, \\
\log \left( \frac{1 - \chi^{asset}_{i,t}}{1 + \chi^{asset}_{i,t+1}} \right) & \text{otherwise,}
\end{cases}
\end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*}
\tau^{curr}_{c,t+1} = 
\begin{cases}
\log \left( \frac{1 + \chi^{curr}_{c,t+1}}{1 - \chi^{curr}_{c,t}} \right) & \text{if } \phi_{c,t+1} > 0 \text{ or } w_{c,t+1} > w^{-}_{c,t+1}, \\
\log \left( \frac{1 - \chi^{curr}_{c,t}}{1 + \chi^{curr}_{c,t+1}} \right) & \text{otherwise.}
\end{cases}
\end{equation*}

For our empirical analysis, we adopt the simplifying assumption, that proportional bid-ask spreads remain constant both over time and across the cross-sections of both assets and currencies. We set the half-spreads to $\chi^{asset} = 0.001$ (10 bps) for all equities and $\chi^{curr} = 0.0001$ (1 bp) for all currencies, consistent with empirical finidngs in \cite{Jones2002} and \cite{NeelyWellerUlrich2009}. Under these assumptions, the transaction costs simplify to constant values of $\tau^{asset} \approx 0.002$ and $\tau^{curr} \approx 0.0002$.
